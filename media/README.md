# Audio companion — the 6-30 / Petrushka note

Sonifications of the note's central claims. **MIDI is the primary, editable artifact** (open in any
DAW/notation editor; change instrument, tempo, transpose); the **WAV** files are convenience renders
(self-contained additive sine synthesis, no soundfont needed) so they play without a synthesizer.
All are regenerated by `make_audio.py` (pure Python + numpy).

| File | What you hear |
|------|---------------|
| `petrushka_chord.mid` / `.wav` | C major triad, then F$\sharp$ major triad, then both together — the Petrushka chord, two triads a **tritone** apart ($\{0,1,4,6,7,10\}$ = 6-30). |
| `t6_symmetry.mid` / `.wav` | 6-30 $=\{0,1,3,6,7,9\}$, then its $T_6$ (tritone) transpose — the **same six pitch classes**: the half-turn invariance that makes the stabilizer $\{T_0,T_6\}$. |
| `orbit_12.mid` / `.wav` | the **twelve $T/I$-forms** of 6-30 in sequence — the navigable dual-group orbit (Lewin's GIS). The 8th chord ($I_1$) is the Petrushka chord itself. |

Pitch classes are realized in one octave from C4 (MIDI note $=60+\mathrm{pc}$). Regenerate:
`python make_audio.py`. (The other generators: `make_audio_phase.py` — Note 2's homometric $Z$-pair;
`make_audio_micro.py` — Note 3's 24-EDO microtonal realization; `make_audio_scales.py` — the corpus
paper's scales-and-tuning sonifications, below.)

## 🎼 Corpus paper — scales and tuning (Pillar 3)

Sonifications of the corpus paper's third pillar (`make_audio_scales.py`). The four 12-EDO examples
emit MIDI + WAV; the two tuning examples are **microtonal** (built from exact frequency ratios) and emit
WAV only, so the comma is literally audible.

| File | What you hear |
|------|---------------|
| `diatonic_scale.mid` / `.wav` | the **maximally even** 7-of-12 (the diatonic) ascending — `IsMaxEven`. |
| `pentatonic_scale.mid` / `.wav` | the maximally even 5-of-12 (the pentatonic). |
| `even_vs_cluster.mid` / `.wav` | the diatonic (even) **vs** the 7-note chromatic cluster $\{0,\dots,6\}$ (un-even) — why the all-pairs energy singles out the diatonic (`diatonic_unique`). |
| `circle_of_fifths.mid` / `.wav` | stepping by the reduced fifth (7 semitones) through all twelve pitch classes and closing (`circle_of_fifths_complete`). |
| `pythagorean_comma.wav` | twelve **pure** fifths $(3/2)$ vs seven octaves $(2/1)$ — the same note a **Pythagorean comma** apart ($531441/524288 \approx 23.46$ cents), heard as a beat (`closure_defect_eq_pc`). |
| `syntonic_comma.wav` | four pure fifths $(81/64)$ vs a pure major third $(5/4)$ — the **syntonic comma** $81/80 \approx 21.5$ cents (`meantone_defect_eq_syntonic`, `four_fifths_eq_major_third`). |
